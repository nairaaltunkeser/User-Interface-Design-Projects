What is ISO?

ISO is a camera setting that will brighten or darken a photo.

As you increase your ISO number, your photos will grow progressively brighter.

ISO can help you capture images in darker environments, or be more flexible about your aperture and shutter speed settings.

However, raising your ISO has consequences.

High ISO will show a lot of grain (noise), and might not be usable.

So, brightening a photo via ISO is always a trade-off. You should only raise your ISO when you are unable to brighten the photo via shutter speed or aperture instead.
